# Accounting Automation — Market Validation & Go-To-Market

## Confidence Assessment

### What We Are Very Confident About

- The monthly cycle (document collection → bookkeeping → VAT by 25th → PAYE by 7th → management accounts) is the same across all SA accounting firms — SARS deadlines are fixed by law
- Document chasing being the #1 pain — universal and well documented across the industry
- The software landscape — Xero and Sage One dominate small-to-medium SA firms, QuickBooks is present but smaller share
- Excel still running in the background — consistent across the industry globally and in SA

### What Needs to Be Tested in Real Conversations

- Whether firms will pay R2,500/month specifically — that number needs to be validated face-to-face
- How much resistance comes from firms who've tried and failed with other tools before
- Exactly how many use WhatsApp vs email as primary client communication — varies by firm culture

---

## Will They Pay?

### Small Firms (2–5 staff) — Harder

Price sensitive. The owner is often doing the chasing themselves. They feel the pain but may not frame it as a cost. Longer sales cycle.

### Medium Firms (5–15 staff) — Primary Target

**Yes.** They have staff whose time costs money. A partner who bills R500/hour losing 2 hours a month per client to chasing documents does the math instantly.

### The Pitch That Works

> "You're spending billable time on admin. I turn that admin into automated messages. You just do the work."

Do not lead with features. Lead with the cost of the time they are losing.

Do not start with the Big 4 (Deloitte, PwC, KPMG, EY) — they have internal IT teams and slow procurement. Target mid-tier nationals and independent practices.

### The Real Target — Independent Practices

**5 to 20 staff. One or two partners. No brand name.**

These are the firms who say yes in one meeting. No procurement process. No committee. The partner you speak to is the decision maker.

Find them on Google Maps, SAIPA directory, or LinkedIn. There are thousands across SA.

## How to Find Someone Who Works There

### LinkedIn — Most Direct

Search: `"accountant" OR "bookkeeper" OR "tax practitioner"` + your city.

Message them honestly:
> "I'm building a tool for accounting firms and would love 20 minutes to understand how your team works. Not selling anything yet."

Most people respond to honest curiosity. You are not pitching — you are learning.

### Industry Bodies — To Find Smaller Practitioners

| Body | Who They Cover | Where |
|------|---------------|-------|
| **SAICA** — SA Institute of Chartered Accountants | Chartered Accountants (CAs) | saica.co.za — has a member directory |
| **SAIPA** — SA Institute of Professional Accountants | Smaller, non-CA firms — your real target market | saipa.co.za |
| **CIMA SA** | Management accountants specifically | cimaglobal.com/sa |

SAIPA members are the independent practitioners — 2 to 15 staff firms. This is the exact profile to target first.

### WhatsApp and Facebook Groups

Active communities of accountants and bookkeepers exist on both platforms.

Search Facebook for:
- "South African Accountants"
- "Bookkeepers SA"
- "SA Tax Practitioners"

These are full of independent practitioners who will give honest feedback in exchange for nothing. Join, add value, ask questions — do not pitch.

## The Fastest Validation Method

Find 3 accounting firms on Google Maps in your area. Call them. Say:

> "I'm building software to automate document collection and client follow-ups for accounting firms. Would you be willing to spend 30 minutes telling me how your firm currently handles it?"

Most will say yes. Three conversations will tell you more than any document.

**What to listen for in those conversations:**
- Do they mention document chasing without being prompted?
- Do they use WhatsApp or email with clients?
- Do they track deadlines in a spreadsheet or calendar?
- What software are they on — Xero, Sage, QuickBooks?
- Have they tried to solve this before? What happened?

If three different firms mention the same pain unprompted — you have product-market fit before writing a single line of code.
