# Accounting Firms — What They Do With Documents

Once documents arrive from a client, here is exactly what the accountant does with each one.

---

## Bank Statements

- Reconcile every transaction against the books — match each line to an invoice or expense
- Categorise everything (income, cost of sales, overhead, etc.)
- Identify anything unexplained and query the client

---

## Supplier Invoices & Receipts

- Capture each one into Xero/Sage as a bill or expense
- Assign to the correct expense category
- Extract the VAT amount — this feeds directly into the VAT return
- File or scan for record-keeping (SARS can audit 5 years back)

---

## Sales Invoices (what the client billed their customers)

- Capture as income in the accounting system
- Check that money received in the bank matches invoices issued
- Flag unpaid invoices as debtors

---

## Payroll Information (hours, commissions, staff changes)

- Run the payroll calculation — gross salary, PAYE deduction, UIF, SDL
- Generate payslips per employee
- Produce the EMP201 form for SARS submission
- Transfer net salaries to employee accounts (or instruct the client to)

---

## What Happens After Everything Is Captured

- VAT return is calculated from all VAT on purchases vs sales — submitted to SARS
- Management accounts are produced — income statement, balance sheet, cash flow
- Those reports are formatted and sent to the client

---

## The Key Insight

The documents are not the product — they are the raw material.

The product is the VAT return, the payslips, the management accounts.

**No documents = no product = missed deadlines = SARS penalties.**

That is why document chasing is the single biggest pain point. Everything downstream is blocked until those documents arrive.
