# Accounting Firms — Deep Dive

## What Accounting Firms Do Every Single Day

A typical small-to-medium South African accounting firm has 2–15 staff and serves 50–200 business clients. Every month they repeat the same cycle.

---

### The Monthly Cycle (same every month, forever)

1. **Document collection** — chase clients for bank statements, invoices, receipts, payroll info
2. **Bookkeeping** — capture transactions into Xero, Sage, or QuickBooks
3. **VAT returns** — calculate and submit to SARS by the 25th
4. **Payroll** — calculate salaries, deductions, PAYE, UIF — generate payslips — submit EMP201 to SARS by the 7th
5. **Management accounts** — produce monthly income statement, balance sheet, cash flow for each client — format it — email it
6. **Answer client calls** — "what's my VAT this month?", "did you submit my return?", "can you send my financials?"

### Annual Work (on top of that)

- Income tax returns
- Annual financial statements
- CIPC annual returns (company registrations)
- Dealing with SARS audits, verifications, notices

---

## Where They Bleed Time

### 1. Document Chasing — The Biggest Pain

Every single month the same accountant sends the same message to the same clients:

> "Hi, please send your bank statements and invoices for February."

Some clients respond same day. Most take a week. Some take three weeks. The accountant cannot do the work without the documents. So she waits. Then SARS deadline approaches. Then she works on Saturday night.

**This happens every single month for every single client for the entire life of the firm.**

---

### 2. Deadline Panic

SARS deadlines do not move:

- PAYE due **7th** of every month
- VAT due **25th** of every month
- Income tax — various dates per client
- CIPC annual returns — specific date per company

One accountant managing 80 clients has 80 sets of deadlines to track. Missing one means penalties. Penalties mean angry clients. Angry clients leave.

Most firms track this in a spreadsheet or a wall calendar.

---

### 3. Management Accounts Delivery

Every client wants their monthly management accounts by the 15th. For a firm with 60 clients, that means 60 reports — formatted, personalised, emailed — in the first two weeks of every month.

Most firms still do this manually. One by one. Copy template. Update numbers. Save PDF. Open email. Attach. Send. Repeat 59 more times.

---

### 4. Client Questions That Interrupt Everything

A client calls at 2pm on a Thursday:

> "How much is my VAT this month?"

The accountant stops what she's doing, opens the client file, finds the figure, calls back. That's 15 minutes gone. Multiply by 10 calls a day across the team.

---

### 5. Their Own Invoicing and Payment Chasing

Accountants chase payments for other people for a living — but most don't have a good system for chasing their own. Invoices go out, some clients pay late, someone manually follows up.

## Where Excel Hides the Real Pain

Most firms are on Xero, Sage, or QuickBooks — but Excel is still running in the background. It is where the gap between what accounting software does and what the accountant actually needs gets patched manually.

| What they use Excel for | Why it's a problem |
|------------------------|-------------------|
| Deadline tracking — 80 clients, colour-coded due dates | One missed update = missed deadline = SARS penalty |
| Management accounts templates — updated manually each month | Copy-paste errors, formatting breaks, hours wasted |
| Payroll for small clients who haven't paid for payroll software | Calculation errors, no audit trail, compliance risk |
| Billing their own clients — hours tracked, invoiced from a spreadsheet | Invoices go out late, revenue leaks |
| Trial balance adjustments — exported from Xero, adjusted, re-imported | Double-handling, version conflicts, data loss risk |

**Every spreadsheet they rely on is a feature we could replace.**
