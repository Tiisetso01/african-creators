# Problems African Creators Can Solve & How To Get Customers

## Problems They Can Solve

---

### Construction & Contracting
- Quoting takes 3 days — should take 10 minutes
- Invoices sent late = cash flow problems
- Clients WhatsApp-ing "how far is my job?" all day
- Site progress reports done manually in Word
- Subcontractor payments tracked in a notebook

### Legal Firms
- Drafting standard contracts from scratch every time
- Billing hours manually at month end
- Clients calling to ask "what's happening with my case?"
- New client intake forms done on paper
- Filing and document retrieval takes hours

### Real Estate Agencies
- Manually sending property listings to every enquiry
- Following up on leads who went cold — forgotten
- Rental agreements typed out per tenant
- Maintenance requests managed via WhatsApp chaos
- Monthly statements to landlords done by hand

### Medical Practices & Clinics
- Appointment booking via phone only
- No-shows with zero reminder system
- Medical certificates typed one by one
- Billing and medical aid claims done manually
- Patient follow-up calls done by a human

### Accounting Firms
- Chasing clients for documents every month
- Sending the same "please send your bank statements" email 50 times
- Management accounts formatted manually in Excel
- Tax deadline reminders done by a person
- Onboarding new clients — same paperwork every time

### Retail & Wholesale
- Stock levels tracked in a spreadsheet that's always wrong
- Purchase orders typed manually per supplier
- Price lists updated by hand across WhatsApp and email
- Credit applications processed on paper
- Delivery confirmations chased via phone

### Logistics & Transport
- Driver schedules managed in a WhatsApp group
- Proof of delivery collected on paper, captured later
- Fuel logs done manually per vehicle
- Client invoices created per trip in Excel
- No visibility on where loads are without calling the driver

### Hospitality (Restaurants, Guest Houses, Hotels)
- Reservations managed in a diary
- No automated confirmation or reminder to guests
- Event quotes typed from scratch per enquiry
- Staff rosters done in WhatsApp groups
- Supplier orders sent by voice note

### Schools & Tutoring Centres
- Fee statements generated manually per learner
- Attendance tracked on paper
- Parent communication via individual WhatsApp messages
- Progress reports typed one by one
- Enrolment forms collected on paper

### Recruitment Agencies
- CV screening done manually for every role
- Interview scheduling via back-and-forth email chains
- Candidate status tracked in a spreadsheet
- Job offer letters typed from scratch
- Client updates on placements done by phone call

### Insurance Brokers
- Policy renewal reminders done manually
- Claims follow-ups tracked in a notebook
- New client onboarding paperwork printed and signed
- Monthly commission statements calculated in Excel
- No automated check-ins with clients between renewals

### Property Management
- Maintenance requests received via WhatsApp, lost in the thread
- Lease renewals missed because no system tracks expiry dates
- Monthly utility invoices generated manually per unit
- Tenant communication done one-by-one
- Inspection reports typed in Word after every visit

### Events & Entertainment
- Event proposals built from scratch every enquiry
- Supplier bookings tracked in a spreadsheet
- No automated timeline reminders for event day
- Post-event invoicing delayed by days
- Vendor payments tracked manually

## How To Actually Get Customers — The Hard Part

This is the real problem. A beautiful website does not get you clients. Here's what works.

---

### 1. Go Where Business Owners Already Are

**WhatsApp business groups** — every industry has them. Builders, attorneys, agents, accountants. Join them. Add value first (share useful information). Then mention what you do when someone complains about a problem you solve.

**Facebook Groups** — "South African Business Owners", "Joburg Entrepreneurs", industry-specific groups. Same approach — help first, pitch later.

**BNI (Business Network International)** — weekly breakfast meetings of business owners who refer each other. One membership gets you in front of 30+ business owners every week.

**Business chambers** — Black Business Council, NAFCOC, local chambers of commerce. These organizations are full of the exact people you want.

---

### 2. Partner With People Who Already Have Your Customer

**Accountants and bookkeepers** — they see the chaos in their clients' businesses every month. If an accountant refers you to 5 clients, that's 5 warm leads who already trust you before you say a word. Offer them a referral fee or reciprocal referrals.

**Business coaches** — same idea. They work with growing businesses who need systems.

**IT support companies** — they fix computers for the same businesses. Not competitors — they do hardware, you do automation.

**Banks and business development programs** — FNB, Nedbank, Standard Bank all run SME development programs. Getting listed as a recommended service provider puts you in front of hundreds of businesses.

---

### 3. The Cold Approach That Actually Works

Don't send a cold email saying "we do automation." Send a cold email about **their specific problem.**

For a law firm:
> "I noticed your firm doesn't have an automated client intake form on your website. Law firms using automated intake save an average of 6 hours a week on admin. Would a 15-minute call be useful?"

That works. "We automate boring work" does not.

Find businesses on Google Maps. Find the owner on LinkedIn. Send a message about their specific problem. Do this 20 times a week.

---

### 4. Show The Work

Before and after. Pick one real automation you've built — even a demo — and document it:
- "This construction company was quoting manually. Now they get a quote out in 8 minutes."
- Post it on LinkedIn with numbers.

Business owners share this with other business owners. One good post can bring 5 inbound leads.

---

### 5. The Free Audit Is Your Trojan Horse

Your website already offers a free audit. Use it aggressively. Tell every business owner you meet: "I'll spend 30 minutes looking at how your business runs and tell you exactly where you're losing time and money — for free."

Nobody says no to free. In the audit you find 3 problems. You fix them. That's your first paying client.

---

## The Honest Reality

The first 10 clients come from people you know or people who know people you know. That's true for every service business. Start there — not with strangers on the internet.

Make a list of 50 people you know who own or work at a business. Tell each one what you do. Ask if they know anyone who struggles with the problems on that list above. That conversation gets you your first client faster than any website ever will.
